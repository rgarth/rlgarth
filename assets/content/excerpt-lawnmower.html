The Extraordinary Penelope Lemon

The Lawnmower

Mr Lemon had difficulty opening the shed, grass had grown in front of the tin door and the rusty hinges were stiff. Eventually, it opened and he dragged out their very old lawnmower. Penelope stuck her head in to have a look around the shed. It was a mess. There were cobwebs over the windows. Old, disused tools covered every dusty surface. Penelope wasn't sure if there was a pair of anything. Lots of single gardening gloves, toolboxes with gaps where tools should be, assorted screws and nails in jars. Penelope was also sure the shed was making a fine home for at least a few of the garden's gnomes. She saw one peering up at her from behind a spade resting on the wall. She smiled and waved at the fearsome-looking fellow and headed back out.

Mr Lemon was busy trying to get the lawnmower to start, it always took quite a while. He would pull the chord, and the worn-out machine would almost rumble into life, but not quite. It took all of Mr Lemon's efforts and each pull would make him more frustrated. Seemingly, the lawnmower was also fuelled by frustration. It always started on his very last attempt, right before giving up.

The mower finally roared into life, oily smoke filled the air for a minute as the old motor shook and shuddered. When she was younger, the snarl of the lawnmower and the thought of its menacing blades had filled Penelope with terror, but now, she was far more interested in something else she was seeing for the first time.

A small crowd of garden gnomes had gathered in front of the lawnmower, stretching, warming up and looking very excited. They were smiling, bouncing up and down, chattering together and preparing themselves for whatever was about to happen next. Penelope stood out of the way and watched, curiously. As Mr Lemon began to push the lawnmower across the long grass, the gnomes, laughing with glee, ran out in front, almost getting caught in the mower, but diving out of the way just in time. As the lawnmower was dragged backward over the already cut grass, they would fall back into position, howling with laughter and ready for the next forward offensive. Darting ahead of the mower, having the time of their lives, always just out of reach of the insatiably hungry machine being pushed toward them. While Mr Lemon was getting tired, sweaty and frankly grumpy, the gnomes were having so much fun. Penelope wished her father could see them, as it might have made him feel better about the job he felt condemned to do.

Penelope couldn't make out the gnomes very well from this vantage point. It all looked very perilous, but it was obviously something they had done before, so she decided not to concern herself too much. While she was having this thought, a gnome looked like he might have tripped over, right in front of the lawnmower. Not being able to see him, Mr Lemon pushed the lawnmower right over the gnome. Penelope's heart stopped. She began to panic. Surely, the gnome would be dead or very, very injured. Among the cut grass escaping from behind the lawnmower, Penelope saw something more solid fly up over Mr Lemon's shoulder and land with a small thud on a freshly cut patch of lawn, about three metres behind her father. Penelope, barely breathing, ran to see what it was.

There, lying on his back, was none other than Ted. Penelope looked down, fear on her face. Ted looked up, slightly embarrassed.

'What?' Ted said, laughing. 'It's fun.' Ted leapt to his feet, seemingly unscathed, and ran back to join the scrum of gnomes in front of the lawnmower, cackling.

Penelope was filled with relief, gnomes were even tougher than she had thought. She sat down on the path to watch her father finish mowing. Every now and then, another gnome would come hurtling out of the back of the lawnmower, whooping with laughter. She couldn't help but laugh along with them.
